## Getting Started

### Logging In

1. Navigate to your school's LibreCampus URL
2. Enter your email and password
3. If MFA is enabled, enter your 6-digit authenticator code
4. You'll be taken to your **Teacher Dashboard**

### First-Time Setup

After your first login:
1. Go to **Settings** to review your profile
2. Enable **Two-Factor Authentication** for account security
3. Check your **Classrooms** to see what you've been assigned to

- **Total Students**: Combined enrollment across your classes
- **Pending Submissions**: Assignments awaiting your review

## Managing Classrooms

### Viewing Your Classrooms

Navigate to **Classrooms** to see all classrooms you're enrolled in as a teacher.

Each classroom card shows:
- Classroom name and subject
- Grade level and semester
- Number of enrolled students

### Classroom Detail

Click on a classroom to see:

- **Students Tab**: List of enrolled students with email addresses
- **Teachers Tab**: Co-teachers (if any), with the primary teacher marked
- **Assignments Tab**: All assignments for this classroom

## Assignments & Grading

### Creating an Assignment

1. Navigate to **Assignments** → **Create Assignment**
2. Fill in:
   - **Title**: Clear, descriptive name
   - **Description**: Instructions and requirements
   - **Classroom**: Select the target classroom
   - **Type**: Homework, Quiz, Exam, Project, or Other
   - **Max Score**: Maximum points possible
   - **Due Date**: Submission deadline
3. Click **Create** — the assignment starts as a **Draft**
4. **Publish** the assignment when you're ready for students to see it

### Assignment Types

| Type | Typical Use |
|------|-------------|
| **HOMEWORK** | Regular homework assignments |
| **QUIZ** | Short quizzes and assessments |
| **EXAM** | Major exams and tests |
| **PROJECT** | Long-term projects |
| **OTHER** | Participation, extra credit, etc. |

### Viewing Submissions

1. Open the assignment detail page
2. The **Submissions** section shows all student submissions
3. Each submission displays:
   - Student name
   - Submission date
   - Current score (if graded)
   - Status indicator

### Grading Submissions

1. On the assignment detail page, find the submission to grade
2. Click **Grade**
3. Enter:
   - **Score**: Numeric score (0 to max)
   - **Feedback**: Written feedback for the student
4. Click **Submit Grade**

The student will receive a notification when their work is graded.

## Gradebook

### Viewing the Gradebook

1. Navigate to **Gradebook**
2. Select a classroom from the dropdown
3. The gradebook shows:
   - Rows: One per student
   - Columns: Assignment categories (Homework, Quiz, Exam, etc.)
   - Cells: Weighted average per category
   - Final column: Overall letter grade

### Grade Calculation

Grades are calculated as weighted averages:
- Each assignment has a **weight** (default: 1.0)
- Scores are normalized to percentages: `(score / maxScore) × 100`
- Category averages are computed from individual assignment scores
- The overall grade is a weighted average of all scored assignments

### Letter Grade Scale

| Percentage | Grade | Badge Color |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| 90–100% | A | Green |
| 80–89% | B | Blue |
| 70–79% | C | Yellow |
| 60–69% | D | Red |
| Below 60% | F | Red |

## Attendance

### Creating an Attendance Session

1. Navigate to **Attendance**
2. Click **New Session**
3. Select:
   - **Classroom**
   - **Date** (defaults to today)
   - **Type**: Regular, Makeup, or Event
4. Click **Create**

### Marking Attendance

After creating a session:
1. A list of all enrolled students appears
2. For each student, select their status:
   - **PRESENT** — Student is in class
   - **ABSENT** — Student is not present
   - **LATE** — Student arrived late
   - **EXCUSED** — Excused absence
3. Click **Save Attendance**

### Viewing Attendance History

The **Recent Sessions** table shows past attendance sessions with:
- Date and type
- Student count and status summary
- Click any session to view or edit records

## Timetable

### Viewing Your Schedule

Navigate to **Timetable** to see your weekly schedule:

- **Monday through Friday** columns
- Each slot shows:
  - Class name
  - Time range
  - Room number
- Color-coded by day for easy scanning

## Announcements

### Creating an Announcement

1. Navigate to **Announcements** → **New Announcement**
2. Fill in:
   - **Title**: Headline for the announcement
   - **Content**: Full announcement text
   - **Target Roles**: Who should see it (Students, Teachers, All)
   - **Pin**: Check to keep it at the top
3. Click **Post**

### Pinned Announcements

Pinned announcements appear at the top of the list with a special indicator. Use pinning for important, ongoing notices.

## Messaging

### Sending a Message

1. Navigate to **Messages**
2. Click **Compose**
3. Enter:
   - **Recipient**: Search by name or email
   - **Subject**: Message subject
   - **Body**: Message content
4. Click **Send**

### Inbox & Sent

- **Inbox**: Messages you've received (unread count shown in badge)
- **Sent**: Messages you've sent
- Click any message to read the full content

## Files

### Uploading Files

1. Navigate to **Files**
2. Click **Upload**
3. Select a file (max 50 MB)
4. The file is stored securely and linked to your campus

### Sharing Files

Files uploaded to the campus storage can be accessed by authorized users. Use the download link to share with students.

### Supported File Types

- Documents: PDF, DOC, DOCX, TXT
- Spreadsheets: XLS, XLSX, CSV
- Presentations: PPT, PPTX
- Images: JPG, PNG, GIF, SVG
- Archives: ZIP, TAR, GZ

## Settings & Security

### Changing Your Password

1. Go to **Settings**
2. Under **Change Password**:
   - Enter current password
   - Enter new password (min 8 characters)
   - Confirm new password
3. Click **Update Password**

### Enabling MFA

1. Go to **Settings**
2. Click **Enable MFA**
3. Scan the QR code with your authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, etc.)
4. Enter the 6-digit code to verify
5. MFA is now active on your account

> **Recommended**: Always enable MFA for account security.

### Profile Information

Your profile displays your name, email, and roles. Contact your administrator to update profile information.

## Tips for Teachers

- **Create assignments early** and use Draft status until ready
- **Grade promptly** — students receive notifications when grades are posted
- **Mark attendance daily** to maintain accurate records
- **Use announcements** for class-wide communications
- **Use direct messages** for individual student/parent communication
- **Check your dashboard** daily for pending submissions and today's schedule
